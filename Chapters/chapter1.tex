\chapter{Introduction}
\label{chap:introduction}

\section{Context and Motivation}

Education systems worldwide face a persistent challenge: sustaining learner engagement over time, particularly in domains such as sustainability and digital literacy that demand continuous effort rather than one-off study sessions. Traditional instructional approaches rely heavily on static content, which is costly to produce and rapidly becomes outdated. Gamified platforms have emerged as a promising response to this challenge, consistently demonstrating measurable improvements in learner engagement and knowledge retention \cite{hamari2014does}. However, most existing solutions depend on manually authored question banks, which limits their scalability and increases operational cost.

A second structural problem compounds the first: the fragmentation of mobile ecosystems. Developing and maintaining separate native codebases for Android and iOS doubles engineering effort and slows the iteration cycle for small teams. Cross-platform frameworks have matured sufficiently to address this, but few gamified educational platforms have adopted them systematically.

U!REKA Play4Change was designed to address both problems simultaneously. By combining serious game mechanics with an AI-powered content generation pipeline, the platform produces fresh, domain-specific tasks automatically on a nightly schedule, removing the manual authoring bottleneck. By targeting Android and iOS from a single Kotlin Multiplatform codebase, it eliminates platform duplication without sacrificing native user-interface fidelity. The result is a platform that scales content continuously while remaining economically viable for small development teams and educational institutions.

\section{Objectives}

The project pursued four core engineering objectives:

Multiplatform delivery from a single codebase, targeting Android and iOS using Kotlin Multiplatform and Compose Multiplatform, so that all business logic and user-interface components are shared across both platforms.

Adaptive AI-generated content, producing domain-specific educational tasks nightly via a large language model pipeline built on LangChain4j and the Mistral API, with semantic deduplication through pgvector to prevent content repetition.

Engagement loop engineering, implementing a server-side scoring engine that rewards correct and timely responses and applies penalties for inactivity or incorrect answers, driving sustained player motivation.

Cost efficiency through batch generation, pre-computing all daily tasks during off-peak hours so that runtime requests are served from a local cache, eliminating inference latency and minimising API costs during active play sessions.
